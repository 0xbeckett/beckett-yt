# POST-MORTEM — Content Plan

## The repeatable formula

Every episode is the same five beats. The subject changes each week; the shape never
does. That's what makes the channel churnable on autopilot — the AI isn't inventing a
format, it's filling in a proven template.

| Beat | What it does | Rough share of runtime |
|---|---|---|
| **1. The Hook** | Cold open. State the death in one line ("37 seconds, then it tore itself apart"), then the twist that makes you need the answer ("nothing was broken"). Flatline motif → intro sting. | ~8% |
| **2. The Setup** | Establish what the thing was, why it mattered, and the one trusted decision/component that's about to matter. Plant the flaw without naming it. | ~22% |
| **3. The Cracks** | The mechanism. Explain — in plain language — the specific flaw and why it stayed invisible. This is the "aha." | ~22% |
| **4. The Collapse** | The moment it all goes. Fast, tense, Signal Red enters once. End on the number: cost, toll, time. | ~16% |
| **5. The Autopsy → The Lesson** | The inquiry: it was never bad luck, it was a chain of decisions. Then zoom out to the transferable lesson and the outro/subscribe. | ~32% |

**Title formula:** `The [shocking number/stakes] [Noun] — How [small cause] [big destruction]`
e.g. *The $370 Million Bug — How One Line of Code Destroyed a Rocket.*

**Why the formula works for a faceless AI channel:**
- The structure *is* the retention. Each beat opens a loop the next one closes.
- It's subject-agnostic: rockets, banks, bridges, apps — all fit the same five beats.
- Research → script → voiceover → template edit is a pipeline, not a fresh creative
  lift each time. New failure in, finished video out.
- Evergreen: none of these topics expire, so the back catalog keeps earning.

## First 5 episodes

Deliberately sequenced to show range early — software, business, engineering, product,
infrastructure — so the channel signals "any failure, one lens" from the first week.

1. **The $370 Million Bug — How One Line of Code Destroyed a Rocket**
   *(Ariane 5, Flight 501.)* The pilot episode. Software reuse + an integer overflow
   destroys a maiden-flight rocket in 37 seconds. → `SCRIPT.md`

2. **The Company That Was Too Big to Fail — How Blockbuster Ignored Its Own Autopsy**
   *(Blockbuster vs. Netflix.)* A market leader that had the future handed to it —
   and said no. The lesson: the most dangerous failure is the one you had the chance
   to prevent and chose not to.

3. **The Bridge That Danced Itself Apart — How the Wind Beat the Engineers**
   *(Tacoma Narrows, 1940.)* A textbook of physics-you-can-see: aeroelastic flutter,
   a resonance nobody modeled. Perfect for dramatic archival-style footage.

4. **The $125 Million Mistake — How Two Teams Used Different Units**
   *(Mars Climate Orbiter, 1999.)* Metric vs. imperial. A spacecraft lost to a
   handoff no one checked. Sister-episode energy to the pilot — same "trusted thing,
   never re-checked" theme, totally different failure.

5. **The Phone That Caught Fire — How a Race to Win Burned a Flagship**
   *(Samsung Galaxy Note 7, 2016.)* Battery engineering + schedule pressure + a
   recall that used a replacement with the *same* flaw. Modern, recognizable,
   consumer-facing — widens the audience beyond the space/engineering core.

## Cadence & pipeline (once uploads are unblocked)

- **Cadence:** 1 episode / week to start (research depth is the bottleneck, not
  editing). Batch-script 4 at a time so the channel can post through a dry week.
- **Pipeline per episode:**
  1. Pick failure → gather sourced facts (official inquiry/report where one exists).
  2. Draft script into the five-beat template + beat sheet.
  3. AI voiceover from the script.
  4. Cut visuals to the beat sheet; apply brand motion signatures (`BRAND.md`).
  5. Thumbnail from the template (`assets/thumbnail-template.png`).
  6. Title via the title formula; description with a source line for credibility.
- **Reminder:** account creation + the actual upload are **out of scope** for now
  (see `README.md`) — this plan covers everything up to the publish step.
